\section{Motivation and Objective}

Traditional early-warning systems treat risk-off as a binary decision---equity or cash---ignoring that crises are heterogeneous. In 2008, the USD strengthened (global dollar funding squeeze; Maggiori, 2020); during inflationary stress, gold outperforms as real rates fall (Baur \& Lucey, 2010); in early COVID-19, the dollar rallied then reversed as the Fed intervened.

We build an EWS answering \textit{when} to exit risk assets (ensemble anomaly detection) and \textit{where} to allocate (macro-based routing: Cash~USD, Gold, or MBS). The strategy deploys \textbf{1.5$\times$ equity leverage} in calm periods and relies on early detection to rotate out before drawdowns materialize, maximizing the Sharpe ratio. The leverage is justified only if recall is adequate---missing a crisis at 1.5$\times$ is substantially costlier than at 1.0$\times$.

\section{Data and Feature Engineering}

Bloomberg weekly data, January 2000--April 2021: 1,111 observations, 43 features (7 MSCI equity, 3 FX, 4 commodities, 8 bond TR indices, 18 yields, VIX, ECSURPUS). Target $Y{=}1$: risk-off (21.3\% prevalence), clustered in 2000--02, 2008--09, 2011--12, 2020. No missing values. Proxies: MSCI World$\to$MXUS; Global Agg$\to$LUACTRUU; credit spreads as log price-index ratios.

Stationarity: log-returns for prices, first differences for yields, level for VIX/ECSURPUS; 42/42 pass ADF at 5\%. We construct 20 cross-asset spreads (term, BTP-Bund, HY-IG, EM credit, VRP, equity-bond rotation, gold-oil, JPY strength) and remove 7 collinear features ($|\rho|{>}0.9$), yielding \textbf{56 clean features}. Additionally, 14 routing triggers are built across three macroeconomic domains.

\section{Walk-Forward Validation}

Purged expanding walk-forward CV with \textbf{4-week embargo} (drops 4 observations between train and val, eliminating leakage from $\Delta_{4w}$ lookbacks). Development$\leq$2018; test holdout 2019--2021 sealed (121 weeks, contains COVID). Five folds: GFC 2008 (53 positives), Euro Debt 2011 (64), Taper 2013 (2), China-Oil 2015--16 (10), Q4 2018 (6). Folds 3--5 are thin because 2013--2018 is a low-volatility regime---a data property. All metrics \textbf{weighted by $n_{\text{pos}}$}. Scaler fit only on fold train; AE early-stopping on temporal train sub-split only.

\section{Anomaly Detection Models}

Four novelty detectors trained on $Y{=}0$ weeks only: \textbf{(1)~MVG}---Mahalanobis distance with Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage (mandatory: 56 features, $\sim$360 normals); \textbf{(2)~One-Class SVM}---RBF kernel, grid $\nu{\times}\gamma$, best $\nu{=}0.05$, $\gamma{=}0.001$; \textbf{(3)~Autoencoder}---$56{\to}24{\to}12{\to}6{\to}12{\to}24{\to}56$, dropout 0.15, early stopping on train sub-split; \textbf{(4)~Isolation Forest}---200 trees, contamination$=$0.10. Scores converted to empirical percentiles against train-normals for comparability, combined via soft mean voting.

\section{Domain-Specific Routing}

When risk-off is signaled, three domain sub-scores determine allocation. The \textbf{USD sub-score} (mean of signed z-scores: funding stress $+1$, DXY $+1$, VRP $+1$, Treasury flight $-1$, US relative $+1$) captures dollar-as-safe-haven dynamics during global, non-US stress (Avdjiev et al., 2019). The \textbf{Gold sub-score} (real yield $-1$, DXY $-1$, JPY $+1$, equity-bond $\rho$ $+1$, gold-oil $+1$) activates when stress is monetary/inflationary or the dollar weakens (Baur \& Lucey, 2010). Note: DXY appears in both domains with \textit{opposite sign}---same indicator, opposite economic reading. The \textbf{MBS sub-score} is rule-based: active only in moderate stress (VIX$\in$[20,28], positive term spread, moderate drawdown), blocked in acute crises (VIX$>$30, funding$>$p90, HY-IG$>$50bps)---defensive carry, not a crisis refuge (Diep et al., 2021). All z-score parameters estimated on the development set only. Routing priority: USD$\to$Gold$\to$MBS$\to$Cash (default). Thresholds optimized by Calmar-ratio grid search on CV folds.

\section{Limitations}

Test holdout spans only 120 weeks with 9 regime switches---results are not yet statistically robust. Folds 3--5 have 2--10 positives; $n_{\text{pos}}$-weighting mitigates but does not eliminate CV noise. Proxy constraints (MXUS$\neq$MSCI World, log-ratios$\neq$OAS) and weekly frequency limit real-world applicability. The 2022 inflation/rate-shock regime---the strongest out-of-sample test for gold routing---is absent. At 1.5$\times$ leverage, the system's 35\% recall gap (missing roughly one-in-three risk-off episodes) is a genuine concern that must be weighed against the superior risk-adjusted performance.
