\section{Problem \& Objective}

Traditional EWS treat risk-off as binary (equity vs.\ cash), ignoring crisis heterogeneity: in 2008 the USD strengthened (dollar funding squeeze); in inflationary regimes, gold outperforms. We build an EWS that answers \textit{when} to go risk-off (ensemble anomaly detection) and \textit{where} to allocate (macro-based routing: Cash USD / Gold / MBS). The strategy deploys \textbf{1.5$\times$ equity leverage} in calm periods and relies on early detection to avoid drawdowns, maximizing the Sharpe ratio.

\section{Data \& Features}

Bloomberg weekly data, 2000--2021, 1{,}111 observations, 43 features (7 MSCI equity, 3 FX, 4 commodities, 8 bond TR, 18 yields, VIX, ECSURPUS). Target $Y{=}1$: risk-off week (21.3\% prevalence). No missing values. Proxies: MSCI World$\to$MXUS; Global Agg$\to$LUACTRUU; credit spreads as log price-index ratios.

Features are made stationary (log-returns for prices, first differences for yields; 42/42 pass ADF at 5\%). We construct 20 cross-asset spreads (term, credit, VRP, equity-bond rotation, gold-oil, JPY strength) and remove 7 collinear features ($|\rho|{>}0.9$), yielding \textbf{56 clean features}. Additionally, 14 routing triggers span three macro domains.

\section{Validation: Walk-Forward CV with Embargo}

Purged expanding walk-forward, 5 folds, \textbf{4-week embargo} between train and validation (eliminates leakage from $\Delta_{4w}$ lookbacks). Development$\leq$2018; test holdout 2019--2021 (sealed, contains COVID). Folds 1--2 have 53/64 positives (GFC, Euro Debt); folds 3--5 only 2/10/6 (low-vol 2013--18 regime). All metrics \textbf{weighted by $n_{\text{pos}}$}. Scaler fit only on each fold's train; AE early-stopping on temporal sub-split of train only.

\section{Anomaly Detection Models \& Ensemble}

Four novelty detectors trained on $Y{=}0$ weeks only:
\begin{itemize}[nosep, leftmargin=1.2em, itemsep=1pt]
  \item \textbf{MVG:} Mahalanobis distance, Ledoit-Wolf shrinkage (mandatory: 56 features, $\sim$360 normals).
  \item \textbf{One-Class SVM (RBF):} Grid $\nu{\times}\gamma$; best $\nu{=}0.05$, $\gamma{=}0.001$.
  \item \textbf{Autoencoder:} $56{\to}24{\to}12{\to}6{\to}12{\to}24{\to}56$, dropout 0.15, early stopping on train sub-split.
  \item \textbf{Isolation Forest:} 200 trees, contamination$=$0.10.
\end{itemize}

Scores are percentile-mapped against train-normals distributions for comparability, then combined via soft mean voting. The \textbf{soft mean ensemble achieves F1$=$0.650} on the test holdout, outperforming the best single model (SVM, 0.615). Mean pairwise error correlation$=$0.66 confirms diverse error patterns.

\section{Routing Engine}

When the ensemble signals risk-off, three domain sub-scores determine allocation. \textbf{USD sub-score}: mean of signed z-scores from funding stress (+1), DXY (+1), VRP (+1), Treasury flight ($-$1), US relative (+1)---dollar as safe haven during global, non-US stress (Avdjiev et al.\ 2019). \textbf{Gold sub-score}: real yield ($-$1), DXY ($-$1, opposite sign), JPY (+1), equity-bond $\rho$ (+1), gold-oil (+1)---gold when stress is monetary/inflationary or USD is weak (Baur \& Lucey 2010). \textbf{MBS}: binary rule-based (VIX$\in$[20,28], positive term spread, moderate drawdown), blocked if VIX$>$30 or acute funding stress---defensive carry only. All $\mu,\sigma$ from development set only. Priority: USD$\to$Gold$\to$MBS$\to$Cash (default). Thresholds optimized via Calmar-ratio grid search on CV folds.

During recovery (Apr--Dec 2020), allocations returned to 1.5$\times$ equity, capturing the rebound.

\section{Limitations}

Test holdout spans only 120 weeks with 9 regime switches---not yet statistically robust. Folds 3--5 are thin (2--10 positives). Proxies (MXUS$\neq$MSCI World, log-ratios$\neq$OAS) and weekly frequency limit applicability. The 2022 rate-shock regime is absent. At 1.5$\times$ leverage, the 35\% recall gap is a genuine concern.
